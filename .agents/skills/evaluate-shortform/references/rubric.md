# Short-Form Eval Rubric — v0.1 (Provisional)

> **Provisional rubric.** Mandatory revision after cycle 2-3. The four primary dimensions plus author-discretion are intentionally lean — the panel that scoped this skill (`debate-agents` 2026-05-08) flagged premature-rubric-lock as the highest residual risk. The mitigation is procedural: revise this file deliberately at the cycle 2-3 boundary against accumulated variance.

## How to use this rubric

1. **Read the four primary dimensions below.** Each is scored 0-3 with explicit criteria for each level.
2. **Apply each dimension to the post + brief + catalog triple.** Score, then write a one-sentence falsifiable justification.
3. **Apply the author-discretion 5th dimension at lower weight** for qualitative observations the four primary dimensions miss. This dimension exists so the four primary dimensions can stay lean.
4. **Honor the cycle-1 weighting note above.** Cycle 1 reports are observation-heavy by length, not just by tone. Cycles 2+ rebalance toward scoring as variance accumulates.
5. **Friction goes in cycle-report Friction Notes**, not in edits to this file.

## Cycle 1 Weighting Note (load-bearing)

**Cycle 1 = 70% observation / 30% scoring.** This is not a slogan — it is the single most important guard against premature-rubric-lock. On cycle 1:

- Observation prose in the eval-runner report is ≥2x the length of scoring prose.
- Scores are provisional context, not a verdict.
- Claim language hedges with "this cycle suggests" rather than committing language.
- Any score that feels wrong against observation gets recorded in §Friction Notes for the cycle-2-3 rubric revision pass.

By cycle 4 (post-revision), prose-to-score balance can be roughly even. Until then, observation wins on length and tone.

## Dimension 1 — Brief Fidelity

**What it measures:** Did the post execute what the brief committed to? This is the most direct fidelity signal — every other dimension assumes the brief was the contract.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **3** | Every brief commitment (hook archetype, caption shape, CTA placement, audio choice, target watch time) executed and observable in the post. reference support each. |
| **2** | Most commitments executed; 1-2 deviations are explained or minor (e.g., audio was changed but the new audio is on-trend). |
| **1** | Half executed, half drifted; deviations have no clear authorial intent — the brief was partially abandoned. |
| **0** | Brief was abandoned wholesale; the post bears no observable relationship to the commitments. |

**Falsifiability check:** A justification at this dimension must name *which specific commitment* was honored or broken. "Hook was strong" fails. "Brief committed to credential-flash hook delivered in 0-1.5s; post delivered credential-flash at 0:02-0:04" passes.

## Dimension 2 — Hook Strength vs Platform Intel

**What it measures:** Did the executed opening 1-3s match a catalog archetype with sufficient sample size, or did it execute something the catalog hasn't yet learned?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **3** | Hook matches a catalog archetype with ≥3 prior occurrences AND was delivered within the catalog's per-platform timing window. (Equivalent of hook-strength-agent verdict MATCH.) |
| **2** | Hook matches a catalog archetype but execution slipped on timing or visual delivery. (Equivalent of PARTIAL_MATCH.) |
| **1** | Hook is a novel archetype not in the catalog — has a name and identifying signal but n=1 sample so far. (Equivalent of NOVEL_ARCHETYPE.) Catalog refresh recommended. |
| **0** | Hook misses both the catalog and any structurally recognizable archetype. (Equivalent of MISMATCH.) Brief failed at the hook contract. |

**Falsifiability check:** The justification names the catalog archetype matched (or names the novel one), uses the catalog sample size, and references the post URL with timestamp.

**Catalog freshness caveat:** A MATCH against an archetype with `LOW_SAMPLE` (n=3-7) is directional. A MATCH against `INSUFFICIENT_DATA` (n<3) is not yet a pattern confirmation. Reflect this in your justification language — "this cycle confirms" requires `OK` (n≥8) sample; otherwise hedge to "this cycle adds to."

## Dimension 3 — Pattern-Log Entry Shape

**What it measures:** Does the cycle yield a pattern claim that future cycles can diff against, or is the observation too noisy / too uniform to extract signal?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **3** | Cycle yields a refutable pattern claim in canonical 4-line shape (claim / evidence / refutability / expiry); evidence has reference; refutability names an observable counter-example; expiry is a timeframe or condition. |
| **2** | Cycle yields a pattern claim in canonical shape but one of: refutability is borderline observable, expiry hedges with a soft condition, evidence is light on reference. Workable but not crisp. |
| **1** | No refutable pattern extractable — observation was too uniform (brief and post agreed entirely with no surprising delta) or too noisy (post engagement below sample threshold). Pattern Extractor outputs the No Pattern Branch with reasoning. |
| **0** | Cycle's pattern entry violates the canonical shape (free-form prose, missing lines, untestable claim). Critic rubric #3 fails the artifact. |

**Falsifiability check:** Score 3 requires the refutability line to name a specific scenario whose presence in a future cycle would invalidate the claim. "Future data may differ" reduces to score 2 at best, often score 0.

---

## Dimension 4 — Platform-Signal Freshness Flag

**What it measures:** Was the catalog being scored against actually fresh when the brief shipped? Stale catalogs produce hollow eval — the brief was reasoning against a fading reference.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **3** | Catalog `mechanics_sources_verified[]` and `trend_signals_date` were both within freshness windows (mechanics ≤90d, trends ≤14d) when the brief shipped. Eval is grounded in current platform reality. |
| **2** | One window stale at brief-ship time; post-ship variance plausibly traces to that staleness. Justification names which window and what specifically was stale. |
| **1** | Both windows stale at brief-ship time; eval is essentially against a historical reference. Pattern claims should hedge accordingly. |
| **0** | Catalog was already flagged stale and brief shipped anyway. Process failure upstream — the eval is recording rather than scoring at this point. Recommendation: catalog refresh before next brief. |

**Falsifiability check:** The justification uses specific catalog `last_updated` dates and the brief publication date.

---

## Dimension 5 — Author Discretion (Lower Weight)

**What it measures:** Qualitative observations the four primary dimensions structurally miss. Examples: "the audio choice was on-trend in a way the catalog hadn't yet documented," "the post leveraged a platform feature the brief didn't anticipate (e.g., TikTok's stitch starting to surface this archetype)."

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **3** | Distinctive qualitative observation that materially changes how the cycle should be read; observation has reference; observation cannot be relocated to dimensions 1-4 without losing meaning. |
| **2** | Useful qualitative observation, but partially captured by another dimension's justification. |
| **1** | Observation is interesting but not load-bearing for the cycle's pattern claim. |
| **0** | No observation worth recording at this dimension. |

**Weight caveat:** Author discretion exists so the four primary dimensions can stay lean. Do not let it become a backdoor to score what the primary rubric refused to grade — that's a sign the primary rubric needs revision (record in §Friction Notes), not that author discretion should fill the gap.

## Friction Notes — How to feed the cycle-2-3 revision pass

When a dimension feels wrong, the channel is the cycle report's §Friction Notes (in `eval-runner-agent`'s output). Examples of useful friction:

- "Dimension 2 score 2 felt forced — the hook delivered exactly to spec but the catalog's archetype definition is ambiguous about visual vs verbal delivery."
- "Dimension 3 had no refutable pattern available; cycle would have been more useful if rubric had a 'process learning' branch."
- "Dimension 4 freshness was 3, but the catalog's sample size for this archetype was so low (n=3) that freshness barely mattered. Should sample size be its own dimension?"

The operator reviews accumulated friction notes at the cycle-2-3 boundary and runs the revision pass. The revision pass produces v0.2 and updates this file.

## Anti-patterns (rubric authors / future revisers)

- **Pre-emptively editing v0.1 to fix what looks wrong on cycle 1.** Single calibration pair. Record friction; revise at the boundary.
- **Adding a 6th primary dimension** without removing or merging an existing one. Keeping the rubric lean is part of the design.
- **Removing the cycle-1 70/30 weighting** at the cycle-2-3 revision pass. The weighting can scale (90/10 → 70/30 → balanced) but shouldn't disappear until cycle ≥4.
- **Locking rubric_version at 0.1 indefinitely.** The revision pass is mandatory at cycle 2-3 — that's the point of the provisional status.
