# Mode: Partnership-Sell

For B2B partnership, integration, co-marketing, or referral outreach. Peer-to-peer, not sales.

## Core Positioning Principle

**Partnership outreach is not sales outreach.** The register, proof, and CTAs are different. You're not asking for their money — you're proposing mutual value.

**Symmetry matters.** The prospect should feel that you're a peer with something to bring, not a smaller party asking for access to their audience. If the power balance is skewed, be upfront about why this is worth their time.

## When to Use Partnership-Sell Mode

- Proposing an integration between your product and theirs
- Co-marketing / webinar / joint content
- Referral partnership (you send them warm leads, they send you warm leads)
- Newsletter swaps / audience exchanges
- Guest appearance on their podcast / content
- Distribution partnership (they sell/recommend your product to their customers)

## Offer Shape

| Offer | Signals |
|-------|---------|
| **Joint customer validation** | "We have 3 mutual customers — want to compare what they're using us both for?" |
| **Integration proof** | "Users asked us for [their product] integration. Here's a working prototype. Want to make it official?" |
| **Co-marketing** | "We write for the same audience — want to collaborate on [specific asset]?" |
| **Referral flow** | "We route [specific out-of-scope use case] away — they'd be better served by you. Want a referral pipe?" |
| **Guest content** | "Your take on [specific topic] would make a great piece for our audience — want to do [specific format]?" |

## CTA Vocabulary (partnership-sell)

**Tier 1-2 (touch 1):**
- "Worth a short compare-notes call?"
- "Want me to send the short brief?"
- "Open to exploring this?"
- "Curious if the timing works on your side too."

**Tier 3 (touch 2):**
- "20 min next week to scope this?"
- "Want me to ping your [partnerships/BD] person with the brief?"

## Proof Shapes for Partnership-Sell

From strongest to weakest:

1. **Mutual customer evidence.** "We share [X] customers including [names, if public]. Common thread: [specific use case]."
2. **Built integration as proof of seriousness.** "Prototyped the integration — here's a 2-min loom. Usage is already non-zero on our side."
3. **Audience proof.** "[X] newsletter subscribers / [Y] MAU in [specific segment that overlaps with theirs]."
4. **Prior successful partnership.** "Did [same thing] with [named partner] — here's what came out of it: [specific metric]."

Avoid:
- "We're a leader in [space]" (self-claim)
- "This would be a great partnership" (vague)
- "Many of our users want [their product]" (soft signal)

## Trigger Signals for Partnership-Sell

| Signal | Partnership opportunity |
|--------|-------------------------|
| They announce a new integration | Integration ecosystem is active; window for yours |
| They publish about your category | Thought-alignment signal |
| Mutual user posts about using both | Integration proof, warm intro setup |
| Their partnership page updates | Active partnership program |
| They sponsor an event you attended | Budget for partnerships, co-events |
| Their customer hits a limitation | Referral opportunity |

## Angle Patterns

### "Mutual customer evidence" angle
"We share [N] customers including [named]. When I dug into what they use both of us for, the common thread was [specific workflow]. A lightweight integration would make that workflow 10x less painful — mind if I send the brief?"

### "Prototype built, asking to make it official" angle
"Users kept asking for [their product] integration, so we built a prototype last week. Here's a 90-sec loom: [link]. Usage is already non-zero. Want to make it official / do a joint launch?"

### "Audience overlap" angle
"Our newsletter hits [segment] — [N] subscribers, [open rate]. You write for a very similar crowd on [topic]. Would love to trade audiences / swap guest posts / do a joint piece — what's the lowest-lift version that'd work for you?"

### "Referral flow" angle
"We get regular inbound for [use case you don't serve]. Right now those leads die. You'd be a better fit. Open to a simple referral flow so we stop wasting them?"

## Register and Tone

Partnership outreach should read as **peer-to-peer among professionals who are not in a sales relationship**.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Want to explore this?" | "Would you be interested in partnering?" |
| "Happy to scope it on a call" | "Let's get on a call to discuss our partnership proposal" |
| "Sharing the brief here" | "Attached is the partnership proposal deck" |
| "Curious what you think" | "Please review the attached MOU" |

## Anti-Patterns (partnership-sell)

- **Pitching as if it were sales.** "We have a great partnership program..." — kills the register.
- **Long formal decks in touch 1.** A brief or a loom works; a 20-slide deck doesn't.
- **Asking for their audience without offering yours.** Partnerships are symmetric; one-way asks read as extraction.
- **"Let's hop on a call to discuss."** Too open-ended. Scope the conversation.
- **Generic "partnership opportunity."** What kind? Integration, content, referral, co-selling? Be specific.
- **Name-dropping fake overlap.** "We have many mutual customers" without naming any is weak; either name them or don't claim it.
- **Bypassing their partnership process when one exists.** If they have a partnerships@ email or a form, use it AFTER a warm first-touch, not instead of one.

## Example (partnership-sell, signal 4, email touch 1)

```
Subject: ramp integration

Hi Sarah,

Saw you shipped the Brex export feature last month — noticed a lot of our users run Ramp alongside Brex for different entities.

We've had 4 mutual customers ping us asking for a direct sync. Prototyped it last week — here's a 90-sec loom of it working with test data: [link].

Usage is non-zero on the demo. Worth making it official? Happy to send the short brief and scope a 20-min on what shipping this would look like for your side.

Thanks,
Mike
```

Notes:
- Signal 4 (specific product announcement + inferred user pattern)
- Mutual customer proof (4 named pings)
- Built-prototype proof of seriousness
- Peer register ("worth making it official?")
- Specific, low-friction next step
- Not a sales pitch — reads as BD/partnership
