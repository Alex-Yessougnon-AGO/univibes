# Mode: Community-Sell

For community-led outreach, Build-in-Public, or value-first outreach where the "sell" is long-horizon and indirect.

## Core Positioning Principle

**Value-first. No immediate ask.**

Community-sell works on a different timeline than the other modes. You're not trying to close a deal in touch 3 — you're establishing value, showing up consistently, and letting the long-tail effect drive growth. CTAs are minimal or absent; the ask (if any) is usually "try this thing I made" or "let me know if it's helpful."

**Helpful specific things > generic warmth.** Generic "I'd love to connect" messages die in community contexts just like they die in B2B. The specific helpful action is the whole play.

## When to Use Community-Sell Mode

- Reaching out after someone engaged with your public content
- Sending a helpful resource to someone who just asked a question in a community
- Building relationships in niche communities (Indie Hackers, targeted Slacks, Discord servers)
- Build-in-Public interactions where the outreach is organic to your public work
- Responding to someone who followed/subbed with a warm, non-sales touch

## Offer Shape

| Offer | Effort | When |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Custom teardown / loom of their thing** | 10-20 min | They shared a project publicly and you have real input |
| **Specific resource** | Reusable | They asked a question you've answered elsewhere |
| **Intro to someone relevant** | 0-5 min | You know someone they'd benefit from meeting |
| **Helpful answer** | 5-15 min | A community post where a substantive reply is the whole value |
| **Try-my-thing with low-pressure ask** | Varies | They're in your exact target and your thing is relevant |

## CTA Vocabulary (community-sell)

**Tier 0-1 (touch 1):**
- "Hope this is helpful — no need to reply unless it is."
- "Just wanted to share this in case it's useful."
- "Sending it without an ask — if you want to try it, link's here."
- "No need to respond — just thought you'd want to see this."

**No CTA at all is often correct.** Community-sell often means sending value with zero ask attached. The long-game is that they remember you were helpful when the time comes.

## Proof Shapes for Community-Sell

Mostly irrelevant — the helpful thing IS the proof. If you sent a good teardown, the teardown shows your competence. If you answered a question well, the answer shows your expertise.

If proof is needed:
1. **Your public work** — "I wrote about this [here]" / "Made a tool for exactly this: [link]"
2. **Your track record in this community** — "I've been in [community] for 3 years, usually hanging out in [channel]"
3. **Self-deprecating credentialing** — "Might be wrong about this, but" / "Could be missing something, but here's what I'd try"

Avoid:
- Formal credentials in casual community context ("As a certified consultant...")
- Named client logo drops in a peer community
- Pitches disguised as value

## Trigger Signals for Community-Sell

| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| They posted a question in your area | Reply in-thread with substance; DM only if DM adds value over public reply |
| They shared a project and tagged a topic you know | DM with specific feedback / teardown offer |
| They followed you / joined your community | Welcome DM, not a pitch |
| They replied to your content thoughtfully | Acknowledge + extend the thread, not a pitch |
| They're a peer in your space | Compare-notes outreach, not vendor outreach |

## Angle Patterns

### "Specific helpful thing" angle
"Saw your post about [topic]. Ran into the same thing — here's what worked for me: [specific answer or link]. No need to reply, just wanted to share."

### "Loom teardown, no ask" angle
"Watched your demo of [their project]. Made a 3-min loom with a couple of thoughts on [specific piece]: [link]. No ask on your side — if any of it resonates, you know where to find me."

### "Intro offer" angle
"Saw you're exploring [topic]. [Name] at [company] did the exact same thing last year — want me to intro? No pressure either way."

### "Warm-no-ask welcome" angle
"Thanks for following / Thanks for the thoughtful reply on [thread]. Here's [small specific thing] that you might find useful given your interest in [topic]. No need to reply."

## Register and Tone

Community-sell is the most informal of the modes. Casual register, short messages, often no salutation or sign-off in platforms that don't demand them.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "hey — saw your post. here's what worked for me: [link]" | "Hi Sarah, I came across your post and wanted to share..." |
| "no ask — just thought you'd want this" | "Looking forward to hearing your thoughts" |
| "might be wrong but here's my take" | "As an expert in this space..." |
| (no sign-off, just first name) | "Best regards, [Full Name]" |

## Anti-Patterns (community-sell)

- **Hidden sales pitch.** If you're reaching out to "be helpful" but your real intent is a pitch, readers can smell it. Either be genuinely helpful with no ask OR switch to a different mode and be upfront.
- **Formal register in casual community context.** A LinkedIn-style message in an indie-hacker Discord reads as spam.
- **Long messages.** Community DMs should be short. 2-4 sentences.
- **Following up if no reply.** Community-sell is zero-pressure. If they don't reply, let it go. Following up twice on a "just wanted to share this" message reverses the no-ask positioning.
- **Public-then-DM same message.** If you replied in-thread, don't DM the same content. Either one or the other.
- **Pitching in a community where pitches are against the norm.** Most communities have explicit or implicit rules. Violating them torches your reputation for a one-shot gain.
- **Using community-sell as a stealth lead source.** If you're harvesting DMs for sales, you'll eventually get called out. Play the long game genuinely or switch modes.

## Example (community-sell, signal 5, DM touch 1)

```
hey sarah — saw your IH post about attribution dashboards. ran into the exact same wall last year with a project. wrote up what worked: [link].

if any of it maps to your setup, you'll see it. no need to reply.

— mike
```

Notes:
- Lowercase, no salutation, short
- Signal 5 (specific IH post reference)
- Value-first (wrote up what worked)
- No ask at all — explicitly "no need to reply"
- Sign-off is minimal
