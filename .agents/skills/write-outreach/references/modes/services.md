# Mode: Services-Sell

For consulting, agency, freelance, or productized-service outreach. Drawn from framework's AI consulting playbook and adapted.

## Core Positioning Principle

**Price what you solve, not what you build.**

The same technical work (building a workflow, writing code, configuring a system) can be worth $500 or $50,000 depending on what business problem it addresses. The positioning of the outreach should emphasize the problem's cost to the prospect, not the effort required by you.

**Revenue-generation problems > operational-efficiency problems.**

If you can solve a problem that's close to revenue (lead response time, conversion, retention, pricing leakage), the prospect will pay significantly more than if you solve a problem that's back-office (expense reporting, internal dashboards, data cleanup). Same work. Different money. **Always probe for the revenue-adjacent problem first.**

## Offer Shape

Services-sell offers work best when framed as **diagnostic → deliverable → outcome**, not "I'll do hourly work."

### The Diagnostic Shape (touch 1 ideal)

Lead with an invitation to a diagnostic asset — a teardown, audit, or loom — that's free and low-friction.

| Asset | Effort | When |
|-------|--------|------|
| **Loom teardown** | 10-20 min to record | Best for touch 1. "I made a 3-min loom looking at [their public thing] — want it?" |
| **Written audit** | 30-60 min to produce | For higher-ACV targets. Usually touch 2 after interest. |
| **Custom demo / prototype** | 1-2 hours | For deal sizes >$5k, after a conversation |
| **Benchmark report** | Reusable, make once | Lead magnet offered to many at once ("here's how Series B teams handle [X]") |

The loom teardown is disproportionately powerful for cold services outreach:
- Low effort for you (10-20 min)
- High perceived value (custom, personal)
- Acts as a sample of your thinking — the proof IS the loom
- Invites conversation naturally

### The Deliverable Shape (touch 2-3)

After interest is established, offer a scoped deliverable — not hourly, not retainer (yet).

- **Scope**: "First-week deliverable: working prototype of [specific system]"
- **Price**: fixed, transparent
- **Timeline**: specific, aggressive (AI-assisted work compresses delivery significantly)

### Retainer / Ongoing (touch N+)

Only after the deliverable ships and proves value. Not a cold-outreach ask.

## CTA Vocabulary (services-sell)

**Tier 1-2 (touch 1):**
- "Worth a look?"
- "Want me to send the loom?"
- "Happy to walk through how I'd approach it — 10 min, async. Want it?"
- "Want the teardown? Takes me 20 min."

**Tier 3 (touch 2 after interest):**
- "20 min to compare notes next week?"
- "Happy to do a working session if useful — no pressure."
- "If this looks right, want me to scope a week-one deliverable?"

**Tier 4 (touch 3+ after engagement):**
- "Want to put a 30-min on the calendar? [Calendly link]"

## Proof Shapes for Services-Sell

From strongest to weakest:

1. **Named client + revenue-tied outcome.** "Helped [named agency] cut lead response time from 3hr to 5min — they closed 4 extra deals the next month."
2. **Named client + operational outcome + monetized.** "Cut Ramp's onboarding time from 11 days to 3 — that's 8 days × [value of analyst time], around $6k saved per new hire."
3. **Anonymous with specific context + number.** "A Series B fintech in our portfolio cut close time from 9 days to 4."
4. **Your own background.** "Built the attribution system at [known company] before this."

Avoid:
- "Helped companies scale operations"
- "Worked with many B2B SaaS clients"
- "Trusted by leading brands"

## Trigger Signals for Services-Sell

Services-sell benefits from triggers that suggest a specific revenue-tied pain:

| Signal | Likely services-relevant pain |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| Series B+ announcement | Ops stack breaking; lead volume outpacing systems |
| Hiring SDR/BDR/AE | Lead-response or outbound process gaps |
| Hiring ops/analytics | Data quality or attribution pain |
| Public complaint about attribution | Direct bullseye for attribution services |
| Website CTA changes | Marketing funnel iteration — speed-to-lead relevant |
| Founder podcast/post about manual work | Automation opportunity |
| Job post for "marketing ops" | Marketing attribution or lifecycle pain |

## Angle Patterns

### "Revenue-tied problem" angle
"Usually when [trigger], the hidden cost is [specific $ amount / specific lost-revenue pattern]. We helped [named client] fix that."

### "Stack-in-progress" angle
"Series B ops teams usually have one of 3 bottlenecks: [A, B, C]. Did a loom showing how [named client] fixed [A] — want it?"

### "Peer comparison" angle
"The 5 SaaS ops leads I've worked with in the last 6 months all hit [specific pain] around your stage. Here's what solved it in 3 cases."

## Anti-Patterns (services-sell)

- **Leading with your resume.** "I've been building automation for 8 years..." — reader does not care. Lead with their problem.
- **Hourly pricing in outreach.** Never quote hourly rates in touch 1-3. Price the outcome.
- **"I can help with anything you need."** Vague = worthless. Pick ONE sharp problem.
- **"I'm a full-stack [thing]."** Credential stacking. Lead with impact.
- **Proposing a 30-min call in touch 1.** Time ask is too expensive for zero trust. Start with a loom/resource offer.
- **Offering a free audit with unlimited scope.** Scope the free thing narrowly ("30-min loom looking at [specific piece]") so it's cheap for you and valuable for them.
- **Pricing based on effort not outcome.** If you fix a $30k/month problem for them, $5k is not expensive. Price what you solve.

## Example (services-sell, signal 5, email touch 1)

```
Subject: speed to lead

Hi Sarah,

Your post about the team missing 10-min reply windows on website leads — the "by the time we notice, they're with a competitor" line stuck.

Usually when that happens, 30-40% of the lost leads were actually ready to buy. The fix is ~4 hrs of work if you can already see the lead in a CRM, longer if it's email-only.

Made a similar system for a marketing agency last quarter — their response time went from 3hr to 5min and they closed 4 extra deals the next month: [link to loom demo].

Want me to do a 3-min loom on what this'd look like for your stack specifically? No pressure.

Thanks,
Mike
```

Notes:
- Observation references specific quote (signal=5)
- Problem framing is revenue-tied ("30-40% were ready to buy")
- Proof is named outcome + number + link
- CTA is tier 1-2 (loom offer, no meeting ask)
- Sign-off casual
