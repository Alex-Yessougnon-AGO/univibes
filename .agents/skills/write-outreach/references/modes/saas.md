# Mode: SaaS-Sell

For software product outreach where the goal is trial signup, demo, or integration intent.

## Core Positioning Principle

**Lead with the measurable workflow pain, not the feature list.**

SaaS outreach fails when it reads as a product tour compressed into paragraph form. It works when it names a specific, measurable pain the prospect has and connects it to your product as the direct solution.

**Time-to-value matters.** Unlike services-sell where a long engagement is acceptable, SaaS-sell lives or dies on "how fast can they try this." CTAs should reflect that: trials, demos, integrations that set up in minutes.

## Offer Shape

| Offer | When | Friction |
|-------|------|----------|
| **Free trial link** | Self-serve motion, <$100/mo price point | Low — but only if their IT/security posture allows self-signup |
| **Demo request** | Mid-market, $500-5k/mo | Medium — reply signals intent |
| **Integration/migration help** | Replacing a competitor tool | Medium — low-friction for the prospect but high-signal |
| **ROI calc / benchmark** | Higher ACV, needs justification | Low — information exchange |
| **Custom walkthrough / loom** | Complex product, specific use case | Low for prospect, 10-15 min for you |

## CTA Vocabulary (saas-sell)

**Tier 1-2 (touch 1):**
- "Worth a look?"
- "Want me to send the 3-min demo loom?"
- "Try it here — no card needed: [link]" (if true)
- "Happy to send the benchmark data we have for teams your stage."

**Tier 3 (touch 2 after interest):**
- "20-min demo walkthrough next week?"
- "Want me to spin up a sandbox preloaded with [their data pattern]? 15 min."

**Tier 4 (touch 3+ after engagement):**
- "Book 30 min: [Calendly]"

## Proof Shapes for SaaS-Sell

From strongest to weakest:

1. **Named customer + specific product metric + time-to-value.** "Linear's team onboards in 3 days instead of 11 — cut their rollout cost by 40%."
2. **Named customer + workflow outcome.** "Ramp's ops team uses this to replace 4 Zapier flows — setup took 2 hours."
3. **Metric + similar-stage company descriptor.** "Series B SaaS teams using this cut [specific metric] by X% in the first 30 days."
4. **Specific integration point.** "We plug directly into HubSpot — no middleware, no Zapier."

Avoid:
- "Enterprise-grade security"
- "Powerful features"
- "Scalable infrastructure"
- "Trusted by 1,000+ companies"

## Trigger Signals for SaaS-Sell

| Signal | Likely SaaS-relevant pain |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Job post mentioning your competitor | Stack evaluation window |
| Tool changelog rolling out new pricing | Churn window |
| Founder tweet complaining about competitor | Direct replacement opportunity |
| Hiring for adjacent roles (ops, analytics) | Adoption champion being hired |
| Public blog post about their data stack | Integration signal |
| Conference talk about your category | Active thinking about this space |
| Funding round | Budget refresh + stack upgrade window |

## Angle Patterns

### "Workflow pain + measurable fix" angle
"Teams using [competitor/tool] usually hit [specific pain] by [stage/size]. We fix it without replatforming — [named customer] got rid of [specific workflow] in [time]."

### "Integration / wedge" angle
"We plug into [their stack] directly. Most teams at your stage don't realize they can add attribution without a migration."

### "Competitor migration" angle
"Three teams moved from [competitor] to us in Q2 — the common pattern was [specific competitor limitation]. Here's what we do differently: [one concrete thing, not a list]."

### "Benchmark" angle
"Series B SaaS teams on average run [X ratio]. The top quartile hits [Y ratio]. We have the data on what's different — want the one-pager?"

## Anti-Patterns (saas-sell)

- **Feature dump.** "Our product has [list of 8 features]." Reader doesn't care. Lead with one pain + one specific capability.
- **"We'd love to show you a demo."** Vague, high-friction ask. Be specific: "10-min demo showing [specific thing]."
- **Comparison table in the email.** Way too heavy for cold. Save for the follow-up resource.
- **Free trial link without context.** "Try it free: [link]" isn't outreach, it's a billboard. Give them a reason first.
- **Pricing-first positioning.** "Starting at $49/mo" in touch 1 positions you as cheap, not valuable.
- **Using services-sell proof ("helped them generate $X pipeline") for SaaS-sell.** SaaS buyers want product metrics (time-to-value, adoption, specific workflow outcomes), not revenue.
- **"Book a demo" in touch 1.** Demos are tier-4 CTAs. Start lower.

## Example (saas-sell, signal 3, linkedin DM post-accept)

```
Thanks for connecting, Mark.

Saw Clearbit is in your stack — curious if the acquisition-driven changes are causing data freshness issues for you? We've had 3 former Clearbit customers migrate in the last 2 months for exactly that reason.

Most recent one (Series B SaaS, sub-500 headcount) had their ops team rebuilt attribution in 2 days — no middleware, plugs straight into HubSpot.

Worth a 3-min loom showing the migration path for your setup?

Thanks,
Mike
```

Notes:
- Signal 3 (tech-stack observation + role relevance)
- Pain named specifically (data freshness post-acquisition)
- Social proof is specific: "3 migrations in 2 months" + named stage
- Proof includes time-to-value ("2 days")
- CTA is tier-2 (loom offer, not demo)
