# Frameworks: Four-Step Cold Outbound

An opinionated end-to-end cold-outbound framework synthesized from practitioner training. Strategist may pick this framework when the message is **net-new outbound to a stranger with zero pre-existing trust** and the goal is to reach "yes" without tripping sales/spam radar.

**Use this in preference to the generic Observation→Problem→Proof→Ask structure when:**
- Channel is email or DM and the prospect has no opt-in baseline
- The message is touch 1 to a stranger (not a follow-up, not a reply, not warm intro)
- The goal is to mass-deliver a pattern that looks like 1:1 personal

## The Four Questions

Every cold outbound message answers four questions in order. Skip one and the message fails.

1. **Personalization** — buys ~30 seconds of attention. The first sentence is the highest-ROI real estate in the whole message (drop-off curve: word 1 = 100% read, word 5 ≈ 50%).
2. **Who am I + why give a damn** — answers "is this a spammer?" → "okay, but why does this person matter?" Brief identity + named social proof.
3. **Offer** — observation + too-good-to-be-true result + risk reversal.
4. **CTA** — specific time + specific channel, one yes from booked.

---

## Step 1: Personalization

**Length:** 2 sentences MAX. 1 is ideal. Long personalization reads AI-written. Real people are brief.

**Three valid tactics:**

- **Cold-read** — vague-but-specific-feeling. "Love your channel, no BS, helped me get started in [generic field]" works for any creator. Use only when no stronger real signal exists.
- **Voluntary disclosure** — reveal a small fact about yourself ("helped me get started in management consulting"). Builds trust via reciprocity even if the connection is generic.
- **Real signal** — a quote from their post, a number from their funding round, a named launch. Best when available; cold-read fallback when not.

**The friend test:** if a friend saw you typing this, would they think it was personal or mass? Optimize for personal.

**What AI is good for here:** small templated variables only — casualize company name (Leftclick Inc. → Leftclick; Pacific Creative Group LLC → PCG), pull neighborhood from city ("East Van" not "Vancouver"), grab school/university hooks. **Never let AI write the hook sentence itself.**

---

## Step 2: Who Am I + Why Give a Damn

**Length:** 1-2 sentences.

**Format:** "I work with [named client / industry-client in location] to help them [thing similar to what prospect does]. We've done [specific number, e.g. $4,892 in 2 days]."

**Rules:**
- Always **"I"** not "we" — outbound is P2P, not company-to-faceless-dollar-sign.
- Borrow client authority by naming them: *"I work with a 10M-sub YouTuber Mr. X. We made $3M last month."*
- Match social proof to prospect's reference group. B2B SaaS proof for B2B SaaS prospects. Sub-count proof for creators. Revenue proof for founders.

---

## Step 3: Offer

**Conversion rate equation:**

`(ROI × Trust they'll fulfill) ÷ Friction to start`

Drive each lever:
- **ROI** — quantified, time-bound, specific. "20 booked meetings in 60 days" not "more meetings soon."
- **Trust** — handled by social proof + authority + rapport + in-group fit established in Step 2.
- **Friction** — risk reversal + minimal time commitment + done-for-you.

**Template:** *"I will do X in Y or Z risk reversal."*

Worked examples (pattern-derived, attributed):
- "I'll generate $10K in 60 days or work free until I do."
- "I'll book 20 meetings in 90 days or full refund."
- "I'll redesign your homepage in 72 hours, only pay if you love it."

**Anchor to prospect's scale.** Pitching a $5M/yr business that you'll add $100K in 60 days = asking to lift revenue by 2%. Frame it that way mentally and the guarantee stops feeling reckless.

**Source-claimed lift:** 3x top-of-funnel at ~10% margin cost (occasional non-fulfillment), net 2.7x.

---

## Step 4: CTA

**Specific time + specific channel.** Cuts back-and-forth that leaks ~25% of leads.

**Worked examples:**
- ✅ "Can I give you a ring at 3:30pm today or before 12pm tomorrow?" — one yes = booked.
- ✅ "Open to a 15-min call? If so, 3:30pm tomorrow."
- ❌ "Let me know your thoughts" — vague extra step.
- ❌ "Would you be interested in learning more?" — invites a non-answer.

**Optional alt path:** offer a one-click Google Meet link as the fallback to the time anchor.

## The Seven Levers (Cialdini-mapped)

The 7 levers map to canonical Cialdini influence principles. Track which the message activates; aim for ≥3 per touch 1.

| Lever | Cialdini parallel | How it shows up |
|---|---|---|
| Give first | Reciprocity | Offer something small (insight, fix, free asset) before any ask. Creates obligation; lowers skepticism. |
| internal | Commitment & consistency | Escalate via tiny yeses (watch 30s video → 15-min call → proposal). Every back-and-forth step leaks ~5% of leads — minimize them. |
| Social proof | Social proof | Specific clients, specific numbers ($112,482 last week, not "a lot of money"). Reference group must match the prospect's niche. |
| Authority | Authority | Credentials, partnerships, named results. Must fit prospect's mental model (Google Partner > behavioral neuroscience degree for SMB). |
| Rapport | Liking | Explicit (shared dog breed, school) + implicit (mirror their tone, message length, punctuation, lowercase if they use lowercase). |
| Scarcity | Scarcity | **Real** capacity/time limits, not fake ones. Tie to your own constraints ("juggling other clients", "proposal expires Friday"). |
| Shared identity | Unity | In-group language (CTR/subs/thumbs for YouTubers; AI/agents for AI buyers), shared struggles, ethnic/cultural/career overlap. |

## Structural Outbound Example

Pattern-derived. Treat as a structural example, not a copy-paste replacement for the strategist's normal flow.

```
Hi Nick, love your channel man — very no-BS, helped me get
started in [generic field you can claim]. Think I can help
with something and maybe return the favor.

I currently work with [named client / 10M-sub YouTuber Mr. X]
to help them build landing pages. We made $3M last month alone.

Went through your landing page at [brand] and frankly, you're
bleeding money. I'm so confident a couple minor changes fix
this that I bet I can generate at least $100K in the next 60
days. 100% upfront, no strings, 5 mins of your time, and only
if I hit $100K do I ask for a small cap (15-20%).

Would you be open to a 15-min call? If so, 3:30pm tomorrow.
Best, [Name]
```

**Maps to the four steps:**
- Step 1 (Personalization): "love your channel man — very no-BS, helped me get started" (cold-read + voluntary disclosure)
- Step 2 (Who am I): "I currently work with [named client]. We made $3M last month."
- Step 3 (Offer): "$100K in 60 days, upfront-free, only-pay-if-I-hit-it" (specific number + risk reversal + scale-anchored)
- Step 4 (CTA): "15-min call, 3:30pm tomorrow" (specific time, specific channel, one-yes-from-booked)

## Failure Modes

- **Long personalization (3+ sentences).** Falls into AI-tell territory. Cut to 1-2.
- **"We" language anywhere.** Outbound is P2P. "We at Morningside Advisors help companies refine their value proposition" dies on arrival. Always "I" until at least the proposal stage.
- **Pricing in the body.** Never quote tiers in cold. Call/proposal territory.
- **Vague offer.** "I'll book you meetings" → how many? when? what guarantee?
- **Stacked CTAs.** "Open to a quick chat? Or happy to send more info?" → decision fatigue. Pick one.
- **Long-but-empty body.** Real human-feeling emails are short. Length is fine only if every line earns its place.
- **Branding yourself as a generic role.** "I'm a thumbnail designer" → borrow client authority instead: "I make thumbs for 1-2M-sub creators."

## When NOT to Use framework's Framework

- **Warm intros / mutual-connection messages.** The framework assumes zero pre-existing trust; warm intros earn a different opener.
- **Replies and follow-ups.** Use `email.md` breakup template + `channels/twitter.md` reply patterns instead. This framework is for touch 1 to strangers.
- **Internal-feeling sales contexts** (Sales Navigator inMail to a connection's connection). The "P2P stranger" assumption breaks; voice-auditor's semi-formal-peer register fits better.
- **Compliance-regulated contexts** (financial services outbound under FINRA, healthcare outbound under HIPAA). The example's risk-reversal language can run into regulatory issues; defer to compliance review.
