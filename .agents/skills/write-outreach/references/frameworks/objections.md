# Frameworks: Objections / Reply Handling

Playbooks per classified reply type. Feeds reply-composer via reply-classifier.

## The Core Rules (non-negotiable)

1. **Never argue a "no."** Ever. The critic auto-fails any reply that re-pitches after a clear rejection.
2. **Match their register.** Terse → terse. Substantive → substantive. Hostile → brief + professional.
3. **Answer what they asked, nothing more.** Don't use a reply to smuggle in new pitches.
4. **One next step or zero.** Never stack.
5. **Compliance with "remove me."** "Take me off your list" is a legal signal in some jurisdictions. Comply, apologize briefly, move on.

## Playbook by Classification

### not-interested (firm)

**Signals:** "Not interested." / "No thanks." / "We're good." / "Not for us."

**Your move:** Acknowledge, breakup, close the loop politely. Leave a door.

**Template:**
```
Got it — thanks for the reply, [Name]. I'll close the loop. If anything changes on your end, you know where to find me.

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- One sentence acknowledging the no
- One clause leaving the door open
- Sign off warmly
- Do NOT pitch a resource
- Do NOT ask why
- Do NOT propose "one more thing"

---

### not-interested (polite-no with a door)

**Signals:** "Not a priority right now." / "Thanks but we have something in place." / "Maybe later."

**Your move:** Thanks + one optional value-offer (not a pitch) + close.

**Template:**
```
Appreciate you taking the time to reply, [Name]. If [specific future trigger — e.g., "you start seeing attribution drift next quarter" or "the team doubles"], feel free to ping. Happy to send [one concrete resource] now if it'd be useful — otherwise no pressure.

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Specific future trigger (not generic "if anything changes")
- One resource offer, specific and low-effort
- "No pressure" explicit — doesn't push if they don't want it

---

### no-budget

**Signals:** "No budget." / "Budget is frozen." / "Can't afford it right now."

**Your move:** Distinguish timing from fit. Offer both paths.

**Template:**
```
Makes sense. Quick question — is this a "this year" thing or more of a "not for us" thing? If it's timing, happy to circle back when budget refreshes. If it's fit, I'll close the loop and not clutter your inbox.

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Give them a binary out (timing vs fit)
- Respect either answer
- Do NOT discount as the first response (if you do, you've trained them to always push for discount)
- Do NOT offer "a smaller version" unprompted — only if they explicitly say budget is the only blocker

**If they reply "timing":** note the refresh date and put it on a calendar reminder. Do NOT pester before then.

**If they reply "fit":** breakup mode.

---

### send-info

**Signals:** "Send more info." / "Send me a one-pager." / "Email me materials I can share."

**Your move:** Specific resource + one clarifying question (NOT a deck dump).

**Template:**
```
Sure. Rather than a deck, here's the [specific resource — teardown / loom / case study link] that's most relevant to [their specific context, from prior touches or reply]. One question so I send the right thing: [specific clarifying question, e.g., "Are you more focused on the attribution side or the reporting side?"]. Happy to tailor.

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Never send a 40-page deck — send ONE specific asset
- Ask a targeted clarifying question
- The clarifier helps you personalize next touches AND signals you care about fit

**Warning:** "Send info" is often a polite deflection. ~70% don't engage with the info. Don't count on it as qualified.

---

### wrong-person

**Signals:** "Not my area — try [name]." / "Speak to [department]."

**Your move:** Ask permission to use their name for the intro.

**Template:**
```
Thanks for the steer, [Name]. Quick one — is it OK if I mention you pointed me to [referred person]? No worries if not.

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Ask permission FIRST before name-dropping
- If they say yes → reach out to the referred person citing this introduction (fresh first-touch, not forward)
- If they say no or don't reply → still reach out but don't use the name
- If they don't reply within 3 business days → default to "yes implicit" and proceed gently

**Second step (to referred person):**
```
Subject: [original topic]

Hi [referred name],

[Original contact] suggested I reach out about [topic]. Quick version:
[1-2 sentence context]
[1 sentence proof]

Worth a look?

Thanks,
[sender]
```

---

### curious

**Signals:** Asks a substantive question. Engages with premise. "How does it work?" "What about [specific case]?"

**Your move:** Answer concretely (one layer deep) + one clarifying question back.

**Template:**
```
Good question. Short answer: [concrete answer, one layer deep — not a feature dump]. The part that usually matters for [their situation/role] is [specific detail].

One back to you — [specific clarifying question that helps you diagnose fit, e.g., "How are you currently handling [X]?"]

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Answer DIRECTLY — don't deflect to "let's jump on a call"
- One layer of depth — not three
- One question back — not three
- If they ask a technical question you don't know, say so ("Good question — let me check with [specific expert] and get back to you by [specific time]")

---

### qualified

**Signals:** Asks about timeline, price, scope, fit, or next step. "When could we start?" "What's the ballpark?"

**Your move:** Answer concretely + propose specific next step.

**Template:**
```
Short answers:
- [Their question 1]: [concrete answer]
- [Their question 2]: [concrete answer]

Want to put 20 min on the calendar this week or next? Happy to walk through [specific thing they're asking about]. [Calendar link — this is the tier-4 CTA now earned].

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Actual numbers, dates, prices — don't evade
- Calendar link is now earned
- Specific meeting topic (not "chat about opportunities")
- If you don't have an answer yet, say so and commit to a specific return time ("need to check with [team], back to you by EOD tomorrow")

---

### later

**Signals:** "Not now." / "Come back in Q3." / "Revisit in 6 months."

**Your move:** Acknowledge, confirm window, soft reminder — NO push.

**Template:**
```
Makes sense — I'll put a note to check in [specific time based on their signal: "end of Q3" / "mid-September"]. If anything shifts sooner, you have this thread.

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Specific re-engage window (match what they said)
- Don't push for anything else
- Put it on a real calendar reminder — show up when you said you would
- If you re-engage at the stated time, reference the prior exchange: "Circling back per your note from [month]."

---

### hostile

**Signals:** Insults. Spam accusations. Aggressive tone. "Take me off your list."

**Your move:** Brief apology, comply, silence.

**Template:**
```
Apologies for the inbox noise. Removing you now.

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Never defend
- Never explain
- Comply (actually remove them — don't just say it)
- Do not forward to someone else at your company "who might be a better fit" — it reads as retaliation
- Log the prospect as "do not contact" across all channels

**Legal note:** In CAN-SPAM, CCPA, GDPR jurisdictions, "remove me" messages have legal weight. Suppression should be cross-channel and durable.

---

### ambiguous

**Signals:** "Sure." / "OK." / "Interesting." / One-word non-committal reply.

**Your move:** Clarifying question OR reframe with specific hook.

**Template:**
```
Thanks [Name]. To make sure I send the right thing — [specific reframe question, e.g., "is the [trigger they replied to] an active priority or more of a 'maybe someday'?"] Happy to step back if it's not landing.

Thanks,
[sender]
```

**Rules:**
- Don't assume engagement — ask
- Offer an exit ("happy to step back")
- If they reply with more ambiguity or don't reply → treat as soft-no, move to breakup

## Cross-Type Rules

### "I want to think about it"
Treat as **later** — but ask for a specific timeframe. "When would be a good time to check back?" If they don't specify, default to 2 weeks.

### "Send me to my assistant"
Treat as **send-info** with a twist — send the info to the assistant, CC the original contact, keep the original contact's language in the email so nothing gets lost.

### Mixed signals (e.g., "not a priority BUT the idea is interesting")
Primary classification: **later**. Secondary: soft positive. Response: acknowledge both. "Got the 'not now' — if you want the teardown in the meantime, happy to send. Otherwise I'll check back [specific time]."

### Auto-reply / OOO
Don't count as a reply. Re-send the original touch after their OOO window ends. Note the prospect is actually at the inbox (signal for non-mass behavior).

### No reply after multiple touches
After touch 4 or 5 (depending on cadence), send the breakup email. See `references/channels/email.md` for breakup template.

## Anti-Patterns (reply-handling)

- **"I understand you're not interested, but here's why you should reconsider..."** — re-pitch. Hard gate violation. Auto-fail.
- **Asking "why?" after a not-interested** — you're not owed an explanation. Don't.
- **"Just one more thing..."** — the "one more thing" IS a re-pitch. Cut.
- **Sending a second email if they didn't reply to a reply.** Their silence after you've replied IS the answer.
- **Using the reply channel to pitch a different offer.** ("Not the right fit for [product A], but have you considered [product B]?") — reads as bait-and-switch.
- **Thanking them profusely for the rejection.** Keep it short. Over-gratitude reads as desperation.
- **Replying to hostile messages with matching hostility or passive-aggression.** Loses business AND reputation. Always stay professional even when they're not.
