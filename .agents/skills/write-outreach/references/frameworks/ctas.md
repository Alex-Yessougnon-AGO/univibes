# Frameworks: CTAs

Low-friction asks. Tier hierarchy. Examples by mode and channel.

## The Core Rule

**One ask per message. Lowest friction that makes sense for trust level.**

The fastest way to tank reply rates is asking for something the reader hasn't earned-up-to yet. A 30-min demo ask in touch 1 from a cold sender is "expensive" — reader calculates effort and dismisses. A one-line-reply question is "cheap" — reader spends 2 seconds and either replies or dismisses.

Cheap asks win. Always start cheap.

## CTA Tier Hierarchy

From lowest friction (easiest to say yes) to highest (most commitment required):

### Tier 1: One-Line-Reply Questions
Reader can respond with 1-5 words and feel complete.

**Examples:**
- "Worth exploring?"
- "Worth a look?"
- "Is this relevant?"
- "Does this resonate?"
- "Still a priority this quarter?"
- "Making sense so far?"

**When:** Touch 1, especially with low-signal (Q→V→A), or when the problem is ambiguous and you want to probe interest.

### Tier 2: Resource Offers
Reader replies "yes" or "sure" and you send a pre-made (or just-made) asset.

**Examples:**
- "Want me to send the loom?"
- "Want the teardown?"
- "Want the 1-pager on how Ramp did it?"
- "Happy to share the benchmark data — want it?"
- "Made a 3-min loom for your team — want the link?"

**When:** Touch 1 or 2. Works especially well for services-sell (where the loom itself IS the proof of competence).

**Key nuance:** The resource must be specific enough to be valuable. "Want our case studies?" (vague) is worse than "Want the 90-second loom on how Ramp did attribution?" (specific).

### Tier 3: Specific Open Questions
Reader needs to write 2-4 sentences to respond meaningfully.

**Examples:**
- "How are you currently handling [X]?"
- "What's the biggest blocker on [project]?"
- "Is [specific tool] already in the stack?"
- "Is the bottleneck [A] or [B]?"

**When:** Touch 1 with strong signal, OR touch 2 after an initial yes. Invites diagnosis but isn't a meeting ask.

### Tier 4: Low-Bar Intro Meeting
Short, async or "quick," framed with a specific purpose.

**Examples:**
- "20 min next week to compare notes?"
- "Happy to do a 15-min working session if useful — no prep needed."
- "Async ok? I can send over a few questions and you reply when useful."
- "15-min loom walkthrough? I'll send, you respond in text."

**When:** Touch 2-3 after warm reply, OR touch 1 for referral-mention framework when warmth is borrowed.

**Key nuance:** Specific topic + specific duration + specific format. Not "let's chat" (vague).

### Tier 5: Full Meeting / Demo
30+ min, formal calendar invite.

**Examples:**
- "Want to put 30 min on the calendar? [Calendly link]"
- "Happy to do a full demo — usually runs 45 min. Want to book?"

**When:** Touch 3+ after clear engagement, OR qualified reply-handling (prospect asked for a meeting).

**Never in touch 1 cold.**

## CTA Pattern by Mode

### Services-Sell CTA Pattern

- **Touch 1**: Tier 1 ("Worth a look?") OR Tier 2 ("Want the loom?")
- **Touch 2**: Tier 2-3 (resource + diagnostic question)
- **Touch 3**: Tier 3-4 (short compare-notes call)
- **Touch 4+**: Tier 4-5 (earned meeting ask)

### SaaS-Sell CTA Pattern

- **Touch 1**: Tier 1-2 ("Worth a look?" or "Want the demo loom?")
- **Touch 2**: Tier 3 (diagnostic question)
- **Touch 3**: Tier 4 ("20-min walkthrough?")
- **Touch 4+**: Tier 4-5 (demo ask)

### Partnership-Sell CTA Pattern

- **Touch 1**: Tier 1-2 ("Open to exploring?" / "Want the short brief?")
- **Touch 2**: Tier 4 ("20 min to scope?")
- **Touch 3+**: Tier 4-5 (moving into real planning)

### Community-Sell CTA Pattern

- **Touch 1**: Tier 0 (NO ask) OR Tier 1 ("Let me know if useful")
- **Follow-up**: Usually none; community-sell is zero-pressure
- **If escalating**: Tier 2 resource offer, always low-pressure

## CTA Craft Rules

### Keep it ONE ask

Banned pattern: "Does this resonate? And if so, would you be open to a 15-min call to discuss further?"
- That's 2 asks and the reader has to evaluate both.
- Pick one. If they say yes to the first, the second becomes natural.

Fixed: "Does this resonate?" (then save the meeting ask for touch 2)

### Name the specific topic

Vague: "Happy to chat anytime."
Specific: "Happy to walk through how Ramp rebuilt their attribution in 2 weeks — 15 min."

Specificity reduces the reader's "what would this meeting even be about" friction.

### Name the duration

Vague: "Do you have some time?"
Specific: "20 min next week?"

Short durations read as lower-friction. "15-20 min" > "30 min" > "up to an hour."

### Async > synchronous for early touches

"Want the loom?" (async — they watch when they want)
beats
"Want to hop on a call?" (sync — scheduling friction)

Until trust is earned, async options win.

### Avoid commitment-trap patterns

Banned:
- "When works best for you?" (assumes yes)
- "Does Tuesday or Thursday work?" (false binary — what if neither?)
- "Here's my calendar — feel free to book anytime" (high-friction, reads as vendor)

Instead: open with a tier-1 interest question; save calendar link for when they've expressed interest.

### Don't apologize for asking

Banned:
- "Sorry to bother you, but..."
- "I know you're busy, but..."
- "I hope I'm not being too forward..."

Apology openers make the ask feel unwelcome and signal low confidence. Just make the ask cleanly.

## Channel-Specific CTA Notes

### Email
- One CTA per email
- Calendar link in the body OK from tier-4+; before that, text-only CTA
- End of email, not mid-body

### LinkedIn DM
- Same as email but even more emphasis on short CTAs
- Calendar links in LinkedIn DMs deprioritize the message — use sparingly
- "Want me to ping via email instead?" is a valid tier-1 CTA if email is preferred

### Twitter DM
- Tier 1-2 only in most cases
- No calendar links in first few DMs
- "Want to keep this going in email?" is a natural escalation CTA

### Platform proposals (Upwork etc.)
- Tier 2 (specific deliverable offer) or Tier 3 (scoping question)
- Video loom offer is a strong CTA here
- "Happy to scope week-one deliverable on a 15-min call" is a tier-4 that works when the post invites calls

## Anti-Patterns (CTA-specific)

- **"Let me know if you have any questions."** Not a CTA. Delete.
- **"Reach out if interested."** Passive, low-conviction. Use "Worth a look?" instead.
- **Multiple links after the CTA.** Dilutes action. One clickable resource per message.
- **"Looking forward to hearing from you."** Dead air in a sign-off. Delete.
- **Calendly link as the CTA in touch 1.** Reads as vendor; reply rates tank. Save for tier 4+.
- **P.S. with a second CTA.** Doubles the ask and reads as hedging.
- **"No pressure if the timing isn't right"** AFTER a heavy ask. Softens but doesn't fix the friction. Fix the ask itself.
- **"Please let me know at your convenience."** Formal register, zero urgency, wastes a sentence.
