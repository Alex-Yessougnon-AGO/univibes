# Channel: Platform Proposals (Upwork, Fiverr, similar)

Pitching into a declared need on a freelance/contract platform.

## Why This Channel Is Different

On email or LinkedIn, you're creating demand — the prospect didn't ask to hear from you. On platform proposals, the prospect has **declared a need, listed a budget, and asked to be pitched**. The dynamics flip:

- You're competing against N other proposals (often 20-100+)
- The prospect is actively reading (for a short window)
- The prospect has already declared the problem
- Your proposal is evaluated in seconds — top 5% get interviews

This changes what "good" looks like.

## The Core Principle

**The average proposal opens with "I am interested in your project and I have extensive experience in..."** That's your competition. Beat it by opening with something specific to their post that proves you read it.

## Constraints

- **Length: 80-120 words** (longer is not better; shorter gets skimmed)
- **First sentence is 80% of the decision** — they read it, decide to keep reading, or bounce
- **Evidence > claims** — links, videos, specific numbers
- **One concrete next step** — not "let's chat," something specific

## Structure

### Opening Line

Never:
- "I am interested in your project"
- "I have extensive experience in [broad category]"
- "I am a full-stack developer with 10 years of experience"
- Any sentence that could have been written before reading their post

Always: something that could ONLY be written after reading their specific post. Options:

- **Specific observation**: "Your note about the dashboard's latency issue under 50k+ events — that usually means [specific technical cause]."
- **Direct question about their goal**: "On the 'users want filters but the underlying table can't' problem — is the blocker the UI or the query layer?"
- **Proof that maps exactly**: "Just finished a near-identical project: [client, specific metric, link to demo]."

### Body

2-3 sentences. Must include:

1. **Relevant proof.** Something you've done that maps to their exact problem. Named (if public) or described specifically (if not).
2. **A specific opinion or diagnostic.** Not just "I can help." Show you've thought about their problem for 30 seconds.

### Closing + Next Step

One line. Options:

- **Offer a deliverable, not a call**: "Happy to send a 5-min loom walking through how I'd approach the first week — takes me 10 min, no pressure on your end."
- **Specific first-milestone framing**: "Could ship v1 of [specific deliverable] in X days. Happy to scope it on a 15-min call if useful."
- **Sample/demo**: "Here's a 2-min demo of a similar build: [link]. Want me to walk through the differences for your use case?"

Avoid:
- "Let me know when you're free to chat."
- "I look forward to hearing from you."
- "Available for a call at your convenience."

## Services-Sell Mode (primary use case)

### The framework playbook (AI consulting)

Key insight: platforms are **the top of the buying ladder**. Prospects have money, know they have a problem, and have declared they want it fixed. Your only job is to stand out in the proposal stack.

**Tactics:**

1. **Custom video/loom per proposal.** 1-2 min screen recording where you talk through their specific project and show one concrete suggestion. Converts 3-5x better than text-only.
2. **Link to a prior similar build.** Public demo, Loom of a similar system, GitHub repo — anything they can click and evaluate in 30 seconds.
3. **Named result, not role description.** "Built a lead-routing system that cut response time from 3hr to 5min for a marketing agency" beats "experienced in n8n and automation."
4. **Specific scope, not "I can help."** "First-week deliverable: Working prototype on sheet-to-slack alerts" beats "I can help you automate your workflow."

### Pattern That Works

```
On the "tracking lead response time across channels" ask — the usual blocker is that the data lives in 3 systems (CRM, inbox, chat tool) and none of them timestamp at the point you actually need.

Built the exact same thing for a marketing agency last quarter: [link to 90-second loom demo]. Their response time went from 3hr average to 5min. Same stack as what you described (HubSpot + Gmail).

Happy to send a 5-min loom showing how I'd approach your setup specifically — takes me 10 min. Otherwise, here's a scoped v1: [specific deliverable, specific timeline].

Thanks,
[sender]
```

Why it works:
- Opens with a specific diagnostic about THEIR problem
- Named outcome with number and context
- Demo link they can evaluate immediately
- Two CTAs — a free loom OR a scoped quote — both low-friction

## SaaS-Sell Mode

Usually not applicable on Upwork/Fiverr (those platforms are service-oriented). If you're on a platform like G2 or Capterra responding to a "looking for a tool" post, treat it as a Twitter-reply style interaction — short, value-first, not proposal format.

## Anti-Patterns

### Opening
- "I am interested in your project and I have extensive experience..." (template opener)
- "Hello Sir/Madam" (generic greeting)
- "My name is X and I am a..." (sender-first)
- "I noticed you are looking for..." (restates their post)

### Body
- **Listing 8 tools you use.** They asked for a solution; they don't want your tool list.
- **Copy-pasting your portfolio.** Tailor the 1-2 proof points that match their post.
- **"I can deliver quality work with quick turnaround."** Meaningless.
- **No links.** In 2026, a proposal without a link to work is weak.
- **Lowball pricing.** If the post has a budget, price appropriately — undercutting by 60% reads as desperate or fraudulent.

### Close
- "I look forward to hearing from you." (dead air)
- "Please let me know when you're free to discuss." (high-friction ask for their time)
- "I can start immediately." (desperation)

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Upwork
- Custom video (2 min max) inside the proposal converts best for anything >$2k
- Use the "boosted proposal" feature strategically on high-signal jobs
- Responding within the first hour 2-3x's your response rate
- Client history matters — look at past hires before proposing (clients with $0 spent are lower-value)

### Fiverr
- Fiverr is gig-first, proposal-second — most work is found via your gig listing, not buyer-posted jobs
- For buyer requests, shorter is better (60-80 words)
- No video in Fiverr buyer-request responses (not supported in most categories)

### Contra / Toptal / Mercor / similar
- Same principles; each platform has different proposal structure (some are form-based, some free-text)
- Always read platform-specific guidelines — some platforms prohibit external links in proposals
