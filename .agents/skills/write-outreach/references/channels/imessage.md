| Constraint | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Total message length | ≥135 chars (~1.5× the visible preview window) |
| Visible preview | ~90 chars before recipient must open the thread |
| Bubble color (iPhone target) | Blue (iMessage) preferred over green (SMS) — trust signal |

## Teaser Preview Constraint

> "Teaser shows ~90 chars — often the entire message. Write at least 1.5x that length so they have to click to read." — internal synthesis

> "Bury something provocative right at the visible-truncation boundary to force the open." — internal synthesis

**Practical implications:**
- If your full message fits inside ~90 chars, the recipient has no reason to open — the preview already gave them everything. They dismiss in-notification.
- Force the open by **exceeding the preview window** and **placing the hook payload near the 90-char boundary** so the cutoff lands mid-sentence at the most curious point.
- "Provocative" here means *specific to them and unresolved at truncation*, not click-bait. Curiosity gap, not vaguebait.

## Bubble Color (Authority Signal)

> "Blue bubble (iMessage) > green bubble (SMS) for trust. High-income prospects are mostly on iPhone." — internal synthesis

**Practical implications:**
- For high-income / executive targets, send from an iPhone or via an iMessage-routing tool that preserves blue-bubble appearance. Standard API-gateway SMS defaults to green bubble and reads as "marketing blast" to iPhone-native recipients.
- Trade-off: scale (green-bubble SMS APIs) vs. trust (blue-bubble iMessage from a real device/account). Pick per audience income/persona.
