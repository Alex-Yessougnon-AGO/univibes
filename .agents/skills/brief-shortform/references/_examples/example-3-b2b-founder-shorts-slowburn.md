# Worked Example 3 — B2B SaaS Founder, Talking-Head Slow-Burn, YouTube Shorts

Reference brief for a US B2B SaaS founder running a talking-head slow-burn on YouTube Shorts. Founder-mode, market=EN, hero=Shorts, no variant. Structurally different from examples 1–2: Shorts as hero (not variant), a contrarian-claim slow-burn hook (not credential flash or pre-reveal tease), and a justified pacing departure.

This is a **worked example**. Actual hero brief artifact: `docs/forsvn/artifacts/marketing/brief-shortform/churn-ledger/brief.md`.

---

## Setup

- **Angle:** "Your churn dashboard is lying to you — the ledger never does"
- **Brand mode:** founder (technical founder, RevOps SaaS, personal brand)
- **Market:** EN (US)
- **Production mode:** live-action talking-head (founder default; single static setup)
- **Platforms:** Shorts (hero), no variant
- **Polish chain:** none (EN founder mode)

---

## Research signals

- Shorts B2B SaaS niche: contrarian claim 6/11, screen-share walkthrough 4/11; credential flash underperforms (1/11)
- Shorts: loop rate weighted in ranking; tolerance for slower builds higher than TikTok (sample n=11, OK)
- Audio: silent-friendly — 70%+ of sample top performers carry burned-in captions, original VO, no music bed
- VoC from ICP: "The dashboard says 4% churn but finance says we shrank", "I don't trust our own retention numbers"

---

## Hero — Shorts (44s, 9:16)

### Hook (0:00–0:04, slow-burn)
- **Visual:** MS, founder seated, desk lamp key light, holds gaze one full beat before speaking — deliberate stillness as the pattern interrupt
- **Verbal:** "Your churn dashboard is lying to you."
- **Text overlay:** "the dashboard lies" (lower third, burned-in)
- **Audio:** Original VO only, no music — silent-friendly per research
- **Archetype:** Contrarian claim (6/11 Shorts sample)
- **3Q:** V✓ F✓ U✓
- **VoC anchor:** "I don't trust our own retention numbers"

### Storyboard (talking-head slow-burn)

| # | Time | Shot | Action | Text overlay | Audio sync |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0:00–0:04 | MS | Held gaze, then the claim | "the dashboard lies" | VO only |
| 2 | 0:04–0:14 | MCU push-in | Walks the failure case: dashboard 4%, finance shrinking | "4% churn… but revenue ↓?" | VO steady |
| 3 | 0:14–0:28 | MCU hold | The mechanism: averages hide cohorts; one prop — printed ledger page raised into frame | "averages hide the bleed" | Page rustle audible |
| 4 | 0:28–0:38 | CU | The fix: ledger-level cohort view, one concrete number from a real customer | "cohort 3 was −22%" | VO lands the number |
| 5 | 0:38–0:44 | MS pull-back | Returns to opening framing + opening line inverted: "The ledger never lies." | "the ledger never lies" | VO; clean loop to shot 1 |

**Pacing note (intentional departure, documented):** Default Shorts guidance is a framing change every 3–5s. This piece holds shots up to 14s — the slow-burn is the differentiation in a fast-cut feed, and the Shorts sample (n=11) shows higher tolerance for held talking-head shots in this niche. Retention risk is carried by the verbal escalation and one physical prop beat at 0:14. Loop ending preserved (shot 5 mirrors shot 1).

### On-screen text
Burned-in captions throughout (silent-friendly mandate). Overlays follow production defaults — max 2 lines, 3–5 words — except shot 3, where the overlay holds 8s to match the held shot.

### Production Notes (live-action)
- **Setup:** Single seated position, desk lamp key + window fill, shotgun mic off-frame
- **Wardrobe/props:** Plain dark crew neck; one printed ledger page (the only prop)
- **Gear:** Single camera, two focal lengths (push-in is a punch-in, not a move)

### CTA
- **Verbal (0:38–0:44):** none — claim carries; comment-bait is the ask
- **Caption:** "Where does your dashboard disagree with finance? ↓" + link in profile

## Why this example triangulates

- **Hook shape:** slow-burn contrarian claim vs. example 1's credential flash and example 2's pre-reveal tease
- **Production mode:** single-setup talking-head vs. example 1's multi-location live-action and example 2's motion-graphic
- **Platform/market:** Shorts-as-hero, US B2B vs. VN TikTok and US consumer e-com
- **Pacing:** a documented intentional departure from platform defaults — showing how the critic accepts justified departures
