# Caption + CTA Rules

Per-platform caption length, hashtag counts, CTA placement conventions. Pulled from research artifact's per-platform sections; consolidated here for `copy-pack-agent` quick reference.

---

## Caption Length & First-N-Visible

| Platform | Char cap | First-N visible | Style notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| TikTok | 4000 | 50-80 | Hook line + 1-2 sentence context + hashtag block |
| Instagram Reels | 2200 | 125 | Hook line + paragraph + hashtag block |
| YouTube Shorts | 100 (title) + 5000 (desc) | full title | Title carries discovery; desc supplements |
| X video | 280 (free) / 25,000 (Premium) | full | Tweet text alongside video; first 280 chars are the hook |
| LinkedIn video | 3000 | first 2-3 lines | Hook line + paragraph break + insight + comment-prompt |

---

## Hashtag Mixing (3-5 max per platform)

| Type | Count | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Broad** | 1 | High-volume, audience-discovery (e.g., #productivity) |
| **Niche** | 2-3 | Topic-specific, mid-volume (e.g., #devtools, #remotework) |
| **Trending** (optional) | 1-2 | Current moment if research surfaced trending tags |

### Per-platform variance
- TikTok / Reels: 3-5 mixed
- Shorts: 1-3 (less weighted than other platforms)
- X video: 1-3 (hashtags less weighted)
- LinkedIn: 3-5 professional ones (no creator-platform tags)

## CTA Placement Conventions

### TikTok
- **Dominant:** Overlay frame at 0:20-0:25 (in 26s pieces)
- **Secondary:** Caption last line, often with arrow ↓
- **Performant:** Save-prompt > follow-prompt > link-in-bio
- **End-card:** Not used; TikTok ends abruptly

### Instagram Reels
- **Dominant:** End-card (3-5s tail with CTA frame)
- **Secondary:** Caption last line
- **Performant:** Reshare-prompt > save-prompt > follow-prompt
- **Overlay:** Less used than TikTok

### YouTube Shorts
- **Dominant:** End-card + pinned comment
- **Performant:** Subscribe-prompt + cross-promo to long-form
- **Pinned comment:** External link goes here (not caption)

### X video
- **Dominant:** Reply-prompt in tweet text
- **Performant:** Reply > quote-tweet > follow
- **Link:** In thread or pinned reply (not main tweet)

### LinkedIn video
- **Dominant:** Comment-prompt in caption
- **Performant:** Comment > reshare > connection
- **Link:** Often in comments (deprioritized in main caption)

## CTA Copy Formulas

| Type | Formula | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Save-prompt** | "Save this [for / when / before] [outcome]" | "Save this for your team's standup" |
| **Share-prompt** | "Send this to [persona] who [pain]" | "Send this to anyone running async standups" |
| **Reply-prompt** | "[Question that invites story]" | "What's your standup that won't die?" |
| **Connection-prompt** | "Follow for more on [topic]" | "Follow for more dev-team workflows" |
| **Reshare-prompt** | "Send this to a [persona]" | "Send this to a founder who's stuck" |
| **Comment-prompt** | "[Question with a clear answer space]" | "What's your team's pattern? Comment below." |
| **Subscribe-prompt** (Shorts) | "Subscribe for [specific value]" | "Subscribe for weekly dev workflows" |
| **Link** | "[Outcome] at [url]" | "Try the bot at conquis.dev" |

## On-Screen Text Discipline

(Shared with storyboard-grammar.md — included here for caption integration)

- Max 2 lines per frame
- 3-5 words per line
- 2-4 seconds on screen
- Bold sans-serif, white + 4pt black outline (or per BRAND.md)
- Color-highlight key terms — 1-2 per piece max

---

## Anti-Patterns

- **Generic CTA:** "Link in bio" / "Follow for more" without research validation → fails brand-fit critic
- **Caption hook buried after greeting:** "Hey guys, today I want to talk about…" — fails
- **Hashtag stuffing:** 12+ hashtags signals desperation; algorithms downweight
- **Same CTA across platforms** without research-backed rationale
- **Caption that doesn't include first VoC phrase** — wasted opportunity
- **CTA placement that ignores platform norm** (e.g., overlay CTA on Reels where end-card dominates)
