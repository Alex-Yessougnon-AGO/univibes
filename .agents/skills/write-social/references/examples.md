# Social Copy Worked Examples

10 examples: 1 strong + 1 weak per platform (tiktok, reels, shorts, x, linkedin). Each shows: brief input → generated copy → critic verdict (per-dimension scores + total + verdict).

Strong examples score ≥ 35 and pass. Weak examples score < 25 and fail.

---

## TikTok

### Strong Example — TikTok

**Brief input:**
- Topic: A solo founder explaining how they replaced a $4K/mo agency with 3 AI tools
- Platform: TikTok
- Brand mode: founder
- Goal: engagement (get comments)
- Audience: indie founders and solopreneurs who feel outspent by bigger competitors
- The one shift: replacing expensive agencies is now a $200/mo founder move

**Generated copy:**

```
## Hook variants
### Variant A
POV: you just fired your $4K/mo marketing agency and replaced them with 3 tools that cost $200 total.
**Char count:** 86 / ~4,000 (caption hard cap)
**Hook archetype:** Callout Cliffhanger (Tier 1, TikTok) — POV frame + withheld reveal of which 3 tools
**Algorithm signal targeted:** Completion rate / play duration (TikTok §3, Signal 1)

### Variant B
I fired my $4K marketing agency 6 months ago. Here's what I use instead (and what's actually working).
**Char count:** 96 / ~4,000
**Hook archetype:** Transformation Reveal (Tier 1, TikTok) — opens on the outcome of the transformation (fired agency), cut to the reveal
**Algorithm signal targeted:** Search value (TikTok §3, Signal 3) — "marketing agency alternative" is a discoverable query

## Body
The 3 tools:
→ [Tool 1] for copy + content ideation
→ [Tool 2] for scheduling and analytics
→ [Tool 3] for lightweight design

Total cost: $197/mo.
Agency cost: $4,200/mo.

The catch? You still need to spend ~3 hrs/week on strategy. That's it.

**Char count:** 231 / ~4,000

## CTA
Drop a 🔥 in the comments if you want the full breakdown — I'll pin it.
**Placement:** Line 7 of 9 — above the hashtag block; feed shows ~1 line before "...more" but verbal CTA in video covers this

## Format spec
- Format: vertical-video-caption (TikTok 9:16)
- Aspect ratio: 9:16
- Pattern-interruption density: 3 interrupts (arrow-list format breaks × 3) in ~231 chars = 1.3 per 100 chars body; hook is the primary interrupt driver (POV frame = 1 major interrupt in 86 chars = 1.16 per 100)
```

**Critic verdict:**

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hook scroll-stop strength | 9 | Variant A matches TikTok Tier 1 Callout Cliffhanger: POV frame + withheld reveal (which 3 tools). Specific numbers ($4K/mo, $200) prevent generic-opener penalty. |
| Char/word limit compliance | 10 | Total caption ~317 chars, well within 4,000 hard cap. Hook lands in ~86 chars, slightly over the ~70–80 soft window, but the tension ("POV: you just fired…") resolves within 80 chars. |
| CTA placement vs truncation | 10 | N/A — TikTok. CTA is verbal in video + reinforced in caption. Default 10. |
| Pattern-interruption density | 8 | Arrow-list format in body creates 3 format-break interrupts. Caption rhythm matches TikTok expectations. Density ~1.3 per 100 chars across total copy (body focuses on list density). |
| Format compliance | 10 | Vertical-video-caption format, correct surface, hashtag structure present, POV hook is TikTok-native grammar. |
| **Total** | **47 / 50** | **pass** |

**Verdict reasoning:** Strong brief with specific numbers, a Tier 1 archetype correctly identified and executed, and a goal-appropriate engagement CTA. The only minor deduction on Hook (9 not 10) is that "POV:" has become slightly saturated on the platform — a fresh variant might out-perform.

**Anti-patterns triggered:** None detected.

- Audience: developers frustrated with slow IDE startup

```
1. Extensions — disable all on startup, enable per-workspace only
```

- Platform: LinkedIn
- Brand mode: founder
- Goal: engagement (comments, reactions, saves)
- Audience: VP Sales, RevOps leads, and founders trying to increase deal velocity
- The one shift: if you're trying to speed up sales cycles, your pricing model is the lever — not your follow-up cadence

**Generated copy:**

```

### Variant A
My highest ACV deals in 2025 closed faster than my lowest ACV deals.
That's backwards from how most people think about enterprise sales.
Here's what's actually happening:
**Char count:** 164 / ~3,000
**Hook archetype:** Caption-first Credential Drop + Contrarian Claim (LinkedIn Tier 1 + Tier 1) — credential embedded in data ("highest ACV deals"), explicit contrarian signal ("backwards from how most people think"), truncation-point fold-peek ("Here's what's actually happening:")
**Algorithm signal targeted:** Dwell time (LinkedIn §3, Signal 1) — high-stakes claim for the named cohort drives "earn the watch"

Not just in time. In discount pressure, decision fatigue, and champion churn.
**Char count:** 184 / ~3,000
**Hook archetype:** Contrarian Claim + Stakes Raise (LinkedIn Tier 1) — "Hot take" signals opinion, stakes immediately named in terms the audience quantifies
**Algorithm signal targeted:** Comments (invites challenge from people who disagree)

When a deal drags, here's what's happening under the hood:

→ Your champion is losing internal credibility defending the timeline
→ New stakeholders join and restart the evaluation
→ Competitors use the delay to get a second meeting

The fix isn't more follow-up emails.

It's pricing architecture.

Specifically: **milestone-based pricing** lets champions show internal wins before full ACV is committed. Faster internal buy-in. Shorter cycle. Higher ACV because they don't discount to close fast.

I've run this on 3 enterprise deals in the last 6 months. Average cycle dropped from 87 to 34 days. ACV went up 22%.
```
