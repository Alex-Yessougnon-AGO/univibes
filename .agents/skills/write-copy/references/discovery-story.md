# Discovery Story Reference

> Alternative social-proof pattern for persuasion-heavy copy. Use when a testimonial, founder story, or case proof needs to build trust by showing how the mechanism was discovered.

## Purpose

Standard proof says "this worked." A Discovery Story explains why the reader should trust the proof: it shows the old belief, the failed attempts, the insight, and the mechanism that changed the outcome.

Use this pattern when:

- the offer has a Unique Mechanism that needs credibility;
- the audience is skeptical of ordinary testimonials;
- the proof point is a case study, founder insight, expert observation, or customer transformation;
- the page is direct-response or belief-building.

Avoid it when:

- the surface only needs a quick proof bar;
- there is no real origin, customer, or mechanism detail;
- the story would require invented drama.

## Discovery Story Arc

1. **Old way:** what everyone believed or tried first.
2. **Failure or friction:** the concrete moment where the old way broke.
3. **Observation:** what the customer/founder/team noticed that others missed.
4. **Discovery:** the Unique Mechanism or new causal explanation.
5. **Result:** what changed, with a named proof point.
6. **Reader bridge:** why this matters for someone in the reader's situation.

## Output Shape

```markdown
## Discovery Story
**Old way:** [specific old belief or common approach]
**Breakthrough moment:** [failure/friction/observation]
**Discovered mechanism:** [Unique Mechanism and why it worked]
**Proof:** [named result, quote, metric, or case]
**Reader bridge:** [why this proof applies to the reader]
```

## Rules

- Do not invent chronology. If the true discovery path is unknown, write a Proof Recommendation asking for it.
- Keep the story under 180 words unless the brief asks for a full case study.
- Use one named person, customer, cohort, or dataset wherever possible.
- Tie the story back to the Unique Mechanism, not just the product.
- The Discovery Story supplements testimonials and stats; it does not replace all proof unless it is the strongest available proof.
